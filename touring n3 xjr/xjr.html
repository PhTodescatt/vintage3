|-Jaguar XJR X308

 The Jaguar XJR X308 is a high-performance version of the Jaguar XJ sedan from the X308 generation, which was produced between 1997 and 2003. The X308 represents the fifth generation of the Jaguar XJ series, and the XJR variant of this generation continued the tradition of combining luxury with powerful performance. The XJR X308 was designed to provide a more dynamic and thrilling driving experience compared to the standard XJ models, while retaining all the luxury and elegance that the XJ nameplate is known for.

|-Engine and Performance

The XJR X308 was powered by the 4.0-liter supercharged V8 engine, which was capable of producing 370 horsepower and 510 Nm (376 lb-ft) of torque. This was a significant increase over the naturally aspirated engines in the regular XJ models and marked a major step up in performance for the XJR variant.

The supercharged engine provided excellent acceleration and power delivery, with the XJR X308 capable of achieving 0-60 mph (0-100 km/h) in just over 6 seconds, a fast time for a full-size luxury sedan of its era. The top speed was electronically limited to around 155 mph (250 km/h).

Paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission, the XJR X308 delivered smooth and responsive shifts, though later XJR models (such as those in the X350 generation) would benefit from more modern 5- and 6-speed gearboxes. The car featured an upgraded self-leveling suspension and an adaptive air suspension system, which offered a balance of comfort and sportiness. The air suspension allowed the XJR to maintain a composed, comfortable ride while also providing excellent handling and road-holding ability in more dynamic driving situations.

Driving Dynamics

One of the key selling points of the XJR X308 was its ability to blend the performance of a sports sedan with the comfort and luxury of a full-size executive car. Thanks to its supercharged engine and advanced suspension system, the XJR delivered a driving experience that was both engaging and composed. The self-leveling air suspension allowed the car to remain smooth and comfortable on long journeys, while the sport-tuned suspension and firm steering offered excellent road feedback and cornering ability when pushed harder.
