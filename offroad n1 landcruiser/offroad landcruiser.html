|-Toyota Land Cruiser FJ62

 The Toyota Land Cruiser FJ62 is a classic off-road vehicle that became an icon of durability, reliability, and rugged performance. Produced from 1988 to 1990, the FJ62 was part of the Land Cruiser 60 series, a line of 4x4 vehicles known for their exceptional off-road capabilities, long-lasting dependability, and strong presence in global markets. The FJ62, in particular, is highly regarded by enthusiasts and collectors due to its blend of modern features for its time and traditional Land Cruiser toughness.

|-Design and Build

The Toyota Land Cruiser FJ62 is a mid-size, full-size SUV with a distinctive boxy design that blends classic Land Cruiser styling with modern touches. It featured a longer wheelbase than its predecessors, providing more interior space and improving overall stability, both on and off the road. The body was built with rugged steel construction, ensuring durability in tough conditions, while the front-end design included rectangular headlights and a prominent grille, maintaining the bold, utilitarian look Land Cruisers are known for.

Compared to earlier models like the FJ40, the FJ62 offered more refinement, especially in terms of comfort and convenience. It came with power steering, air conditioning, and more advanced suspension systems, making it more suitable for long-distance travel and urban driving while still capable of tackling rough terrains.

Engine and Performance

The FJ62 was equipped with several engine options, depending on the market and year. The most common engine was a 4.0-liter inline-six (3F engine), which produced around 155 horsepower and 220 lb-ft of torque. This engine provided a solid balance between power and fuel efficiency, which was important for both off-road expeditions and highway cruising.

In certain markets, the FJ62 also came with a diesel engine option, including a 3.4-liter inline-six (13B) that offered enhanced torque and was favored for off-road capability, especially in regions like Africa and the Middle East.

Suspension and Handling

The FJ62 featured a coil-spring suspension both in the front and rear, a significant improvement over the leaf-spring suspension found in older models like the FJ40. This new suspension design offered better ride comfort, improved handling on rough terrain, and greater durability for long-term use. The vehicle’s full-time 4WD system was enhanced with a center differential, which allowed for a more stable driving experience on both dry and slippery surfaces. The Land Cruiser's manual locking hubs also contributed to the vehicle's off-road readiness, allowing drivers to manually engage the four-wheel drive system when tackling extreme conditions.
