 The Renault R5 Turbo is a legendary and iconic hot hatch produced by the French automaker Renault in the early 1980s. It's best known for its distinctive design, turbocharged performance, and its success in motorsports, particularly in rallying. The Renault R5 Turbo remains a beloved model among car enthusiasts and collectors. |-Origins and Development

The Renault 5 was initially introduced in 1972 as a small, affordable city car. However, Renault wanted to expand its reach into motorsport and create a performance version of the R5 to compete in the World Rally Championship (WRC). The R5 Turbo was born out of this ambition.

Unlike the standard R5, which had a front-engine, front-wheel-drive layout, the R5 Turbo featured a radical redesign. Renault moved the engine to the rear and used rear-wheel drive, a setup that was more conducive to rally racing. This layout gave the R5 Turbo an excellent power-to-weight ratio, making it agile and quick on the rally stage.
