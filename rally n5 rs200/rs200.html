|-Ford Rs200

 The Ford RS200 is an iconic rally car that was developed specifically for the legendary Group B era of the World Rally Championship (WRC) in the 1980s. It stands out as one of the most innovative and distinctive cars from this period, designed to compete against the likes of the Lancia Delta S4, Audi Quattro, and Peugeot 205 T16.

|-Development and Design

The Ford RS200 was introduced in 1984 and represented Ford’s ambitious attempt to dominate Group B rallying. Unlike many competitors, the RS200 was not based on a road-going production car. Instead, it was a completely bespoke design, created from the ground up to meet the FIA’s homologation requirements, which mandated a minimum of 200 road-legal units. Ford collaborated with several notable names to design the RS200: Tony Southgate, a Formula 1 engineer, contributed to the car's chassis design. Ghia, the Italian design house, helped shape the car’s aerodynamic fiberglass body. The mid-mounted engine layout and lightweight construction were hallmarks of the car’s innovative engineering.

Powertrain and Performance

The RS200 was powered by a 1.8-liter turbocharged Cosworth BDT engine, producing between 250 and 450 horsepower, depending on the configuration. For competition versions, power was often tuned to exceed 500 horsepower, enabling blistering acceleration and top speeds above 140 mph (225 km/h).

The car featured a permanent all-wheel-drive system, ensuring excellent traction on a variety of surfaces. The weight distribution, thanks to the mid-engine layout, and advanced suspension setup provided the RS200 with excellent handling characteristics.
