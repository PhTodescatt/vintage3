|-Mercedes AMG 190E EVO II DTM

 The Mercedes-Benz 190E 2.5-16 Evolution II DTM is one of the most legendary cars in the history of motorsport, particularly for its role in the Deutsche Tourenwagen Meisterschaft (DTM) racing series. Often referred to simply as the 190E Evo II, this high-performance version of the Mercedes 190E was built specifically for racing and was designed to be a homologation special for DTM competition. Produced in limited numbers in 1990, the 190E Evo II became a defining vehicle in the golden era of DTM and a symbol of Mercedes' engineering excellence.

|-History and Development

The Mercedes-Benz 190E 2.5-16 Evolution II was developed in response to the increasing demands of the DTM series, which required manufacturers to produce a certain number of road-going versions of their race cars in order to qualify them for competition. The Evo II was an evolution of the earlier 190E 2.5-16 and was engineered to push the boundaries of performance, technology, and aerodynamics.

Mercedes had already achieved considerable success with the original 190E 2.5-16 in the DTM, but the arrival of the Evo II marked a significant step forward. The car was not only more powerful, but it also featured an extensive range of enhancements aimed at improving performance on both the track and the road.

Just 502 units of the 190E Evo II were produced, making it an exclusive and highly sought-after model. While its primary purpose was to meet the homologation requirements for the DTM, the car also gained widespread admiration among enthusiasts for its track-inspired design and extraordinary performance capabilities.

Engine and Performance

At the heart of the Mercedes 190E 2.5-16 Evolution II was a high-revving 2.5-liter 16-valve inline-four engine, which was significantly upgraded compared to the previous versions of the 190E. The Evo II's engine was capable of producing 232 horsepower and 235 Nm (173 lb-ft) of torque, which was a substantial increase over the standard 190E 2.5-16. This engine was mated to a close-ratio 5-speed manual transmission, allowing for rapid shifts and precise control over the car’s performance.

The car’s power-to-weight ratio was significantly improved by its lightweight construction, which helped the Evo II achieve impressive acceleration. The 190E Evo II could accelerate from 0 to 60 mph (0-100 km/h) in about 6.5 seconds, with a top speed of around 155 mph (250 km/h). These figures were impressive for a car of its size and era, especially considering that the Evo II was not just a street car but a machine designed to excel in motorsport.

Suspension and Handling

In addition to its powerful engine, the 190E Evo II featured significant upgrades to its suspension and chassis to ensure the car could handle the extreme demands of racing. It was equipped with a sport-tuned suspension that featured adjustable dampers and a lowered ride height, both of which contributed to the car's ability to corner at high speeds while maintaining stability. The car’s handling was also enhanced by a limited-slip differential, which improved traction and allowed for more predictable handling, especially when driving aggressively or on slippery surfaces.

The Mercedes 190E Evo II was equipped with ventilated disc brakes on all four corners, ensuring that the car could handle the high-speed demands of racing without fading or losing braking efficiency. The overall handling balance was exceptional, with precise steering and excellent feedback, making the car highly responsive to driver input. This was especially important in the tight corners and high-speed straights of DTM racing.
