|-Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI WRC

 The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI WRC is one of the most legendary rally cars in the history of motorsport, celebrated for its performance, reliability, and engineering that dominated the World Rally Championship (WRC) during the late 1990s and early 2000s. This model cemented Mitsubishi's reputation as a powerhouse in rallying, especially with Finnish driver Tommi Mäkinen at the wheel.

|-Technical Specifications

Engine and Performance

The Evo 6 WRC was powered by Mitsubishi’s renowned 2.0-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder 4G63 engine, producing around 300 horsepower and 540 Nm of torque within WRC regulations. Known for its durability and ability to be finely tuned, this engine helped the Evo 6 perform impressively across various terrains.

Transmission and All-Wheel Drive

Equipped with a sophisticated all-wheel-drive (AWD) system, the Evo 6 featured an electronically controlled active center differential (ACD) and active yaw control (AYC) on the rear axle, which allowed precise torque distribution. The combination provided exceptional handling and stability, essential for the rough and unpredictable rally conditions.

Suspension and Chassis

Designed to handle the high demands of rallying, the Evo 6 used a reinforced chassis and advanced suspension setup with long-travel dampers that could withstand rough terrain, including gravel, snow, and tarmac. Its responsive suspension and durable construction made it a versatile machine capable of tackling any WRC stage.

Aerodynamics and Design

The Lancer Evo 6 featured a redesigned, more aggressive aero package compared to its predecessors. It had a prominent rear wing for improved downforce, larger front air intakes, and vented hoods to aid cooling. These aerodynamic improvements contributed to greater stability and control, especially at high speeds.
