|-Vauxhall Lotus Carlton

 The Vauxhall Lotus Carlton is a high-performance sedan that combines luxury and power, and it holds a special place in automotive history due to its unique blend of engineering and performance. Produced between 1990 and 1992, it was a result of a collaboration between Vauxhall, a British automaker, and Lotus, the legendary performance engineering company. The car was essentially a performance-enhanced version of the Vauxhall Carlton, a mid-size executive car.

|-Key Features and Performance

Under the hood, the Lotus Carlton was equipped with a 3.6-liter, 24-valve twin-turbocharged inline-six engine. This engine was developed by Lotus and produced a staggering 377 horsepower and 419 lb-ft of torque. It was paired with a Getrag 6-speed manual transmission, allowing the car to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just under 6 seconds—exceptional for a sedan of its time. The top speed was an impressive 170 mph (274 km/h), making the Lotus Carlton one of the fastest sedans in the world at the time of its release.

The car's suspension system was also upgraded by Lotus, with modifications to the chassis, dampers, and springs to improve handling and stability at high speeds. It was equipped with a limited-slip differential and larger disc brakes, ensuring that it could handle the power and deliver a smooth, stable ride even at extreme speeds.

Design and Styling

In terms of design, the Lotus Carlton retained the general appearance of the standard Vauxhall Carlton, but with several key upgrades. It had a more aggressive stance, with wider wheel arches, a deeper front bumper, and larger alloy wheels. The front end was equipped with a larger grille, and the rear featured a subtle spoiler for better aerodynamics.

Inside, the car offered a luxurious and comfortable cabin, with leather upholstery, advanced (for the time) electronics, and a range of features to cater to both driver and passengers. The Lotus Carlton was a practical, everyday car that could comfortably accommodate four adults, but with the added bonus of being able to perform at a very high level when pushed to its limits.

Rarity and Legacy

Only 950 units of the Vauxhall Lotus Carlton were produced, making it a rare collector’s item today. Its combination of luxury, performance, and exclusivity has led to it becoming a sought-after classic, particularly in the UK and Europe, where it has developed a cult following. The Lotus Carlton’s legacy is closely tied to the notion of "Q-car" or "sleeper" – a car that doesn't look particularly special but has extraordinary performance capabilities. It was a surprising performance car, capable of challenging even the most exotic sports cars of its era while maintaining the practicality and comfort of a four-door sedan.
